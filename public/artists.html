Avran

Having made music his whole life, the co-owner found a love for underground dance music whilst attending raves in his hometown of Birmingham. Inspired by the electric crowds, Avran began producing and DJing during his first year at University, leading to the co-founding of Murky Sonics alongside Isochrone.

Now an AAJA resident, the young artist's output is inspired by a vast array of genres and influences, blending bass-driven sonics with experimental percussion, and even a sprinkling of folk dance, to create a unique style that helped define the Murky sound.

Delian Sound

Delian Sound is a London-born, Cambridge-based producer who makes music inspired by the 2002-2003 post-Garage/proto-Dubstep scene, and features samples from early electronic pioneers such as Delia Derbyshire and her contemporaries at the BBC Radiophonic Workshop.

Boasting releases on heavyweight labels such as EC2A, Dimeshit and Outhouse Sounds, Delian Sound is firmly rooted himself in the UK bass scene with support from the likes of Izco, Mia Koden, Breaka, Jossy Mitsu, Ila Brugal, Riz La Teef, Keysound and Fearless Dread.

Isochrone

One half of the founding duo of Murky, Rob was surrounded by drum and bass & jungle from a young age. His love for bass driven music naturally led him to the sounds of old school dubstep and grime, and later into the realms of breakbeat, electro and hardcore. In his sets he merges the old with the new, the light with the dark and the smooth with the experimental, bringing together influences from all corners of the UK scene & beyond.

Away from the decks, his true passion lies in building a lifelong music based community, where people from all backgrounds can dance freely to high quality music through a high quality soundsystem.

Izzi

With a sound and innovative style that encapsulates what Murky is all about, Izzi spins selections from underrepresented artists and blends UK dance music with more global club sounds with a “strong emphasis on knitting together tight drum breaks and warm wubby basslines”.

The past year has seen Izzi explode onto the London scene playing sets alongside the likes of Ahadadream, Flowdan and Neffa-T at venues such as Village Underground, MOT and Phonox. She’s also begun to establish her name on the festival scene at Outlook, Glastonbury, Queen’s Yard and, of course, Murky Forest.

MontyG

MontyG is a man of many talents. DJing, producing, events, festivals, lighting, tech - he can do it all, and do it well. He started out spinning vinyls with his dad when he was 10, and since then has DJ’d and organized parties across Europe and both North and South America. Most notably, he was a key part of the La Paz techno scene in Bolivia last year, holding multiple residencies and playing at the legendary Levitar.

He has produced several EPs over the past 4 years, with a main focus on liquid and minimal drum and bass, but has also explored the realms of hard tech, jungle and even hip-hop. Monty is the man behind Lock-In events in Bath and was the orchestrator of Wissett festival, which was the primary inspiration for Murky Forest.

Mossambi

Mossambi is a producer and DJ based in London. He made waves with his breakthrough debut EP “Resurf” released via esteemed label All Centre in 2023. This was followed up with a release on Dublin-based label Woozy later that year. He now presents his second EP “The AI Told Me To”, once again on All Centre, backed up by a remix by UKG stalwart Holloway. His musical style has been described by DJ Mag as 'a mutant strain of club music with a distinctly ethereal edge', fusing experimental sound design with bass heavy dancefloor sensibilities. Mossambi also co-runs the club night 'LAYA/TALA', which seeks to promote underrepresented artists within the experimental and bass music scene.

Stack Effect

Originating from Manchester and now based in London, Stack Effect has honed a distinctive sound in recent years, balancing euphoric atmospheres with low-end grit, creating music that resonates in both club and home settings.

Following the success of his two EPs in 2022, 'Could It Be You' and 'Pause', Stack Effect made waves with his debut album 'Iridescent' the following year. Showcasing his versatility, the album traverses ethereal soundscapes, features his signature pitched vocals and emotional synths, and even ventures near the realms of dubstep.

In his DJ sets, Stack Effect explores his trademark ambient sound, but also incorporates dance-oriented techno and breakbeat, alongside more experimental and glitchy beats.
